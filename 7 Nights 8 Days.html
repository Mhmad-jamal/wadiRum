WORLD OF TOURISM

7 Nights 8 Days Program

  1. Amman, Jerash, Ajloun, Madaba, Mount Nebo, Petra, Wadi Rum, Dead Sea
Day 1:

QAIA -AMMAN

Arrival at QAIA, meet and assist then drive towards the hotel Amman in the afternoon enjoy the Amman Panoramic tour then return to the hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 2:

Amman -Panoramic City Tour-Jerash-Ajloun–Amman

Explore Amman, the modern capital of Jordan, situated on 7 hills with a blend of modern and ancient elements. Visit the Roman Theatre, still used for cultural activities, and the Amman castle on one of the hills. Continue to Jerash, an ancient city that was buried for centuries after a 749 AC earthquake. Often called the "Pompeii of the Middle East," Jerash is one of the best-preserved Greco-Roman cities. After lunch, proceed to Ajloun Castle, a significant player in the castle campaign, nestled in valleys. Return to your Amman hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 3:

Amman -Madaba–Mount Nebo–Petra

The day's itinerary involves a drive from breakfast towards Madaba, the capital city of Madaba Governorate in central Jordan, renowned for its Byzantine and Umayyad mosaics, including a large map of the Holy Land. Madaba is elevated at approximately 700 meters above sea level, forming part of the Abarim mountain range leading to Mount Nebo. Mount Nebo, mentioned in the Bible as the place where Moses saw the Promised Land, offers a panoramic view of the West Bank, the Jordan River valley, and, on clear days, Jericho and Jerusalem. After exploring Nebo, the journey continues to Petra, the Rose City, with an overnight stay at a hotel.

Day 4:

Petra Visit -Petra

Start your day with an early breakfast and then explore Petra, the capital of the Nabataean Arabs, renowned as one of the world's most significant archaeological sites. Situated 240 km south of Amman and 120 km north of Aqaba, Petra is Jordan's prized treasure and a major tourist attraction. Its origins date back to the 1st century BC when it prospered as the Nabataean Empire's capital, thriving on trade in frankincense, myrrh, and spices. Petra later became part of the Roman Empire but suffered substantial damage in a 4th-century AD earthquake. Conclude your day by returning to your hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 5:

Petra -Wadi Rum

Explore UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site Moon Valley (Wadi Rum) after breakfast with a 4X4 Jeep Safari to discover Bedouin cultural traces and stunning views of the desert. The unique landscape, resembling Mars in movies like Martian and Red Planet, spans 74,000 hectares of pink sand. Check into the camp for dinner and an overnight stay.

Day 6:

Wadi Rum -Dead Sea

After breakfast at the camp, head to the Dead Sea, a salt lake bordered by Jordan to the east and the West Bank and Israel to the west. Situated in the Jordan Rift Valley, it holds the record for the lowest land-based elevation on Earth, with its surface at 430.5 meters (1,412 ft) below sea level. The lake, with a depth of 304 meters (997 ft), is renowned for its extreme salinity, allowing swimmers to effortlessly float. Historically, it served as a health resort for Herod the Great and has been a source of various products. Check into your hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 7:

Dead Sea -Amman

After breakfast enjoy a free time at the Dead Sea beaches then drive your way back to Amman and check into your hotel for overnight.

Day 8:

AMMAN -QAIA

After breakfast at the hotel drive towards QAIA to take your flight back home carrying the memorable moments of this fabulous trip with you.
